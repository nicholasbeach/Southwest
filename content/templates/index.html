Southwest Airlines


The reason why I want to intern at Southwest Airlines can be summarized in one word: Culture. Company culture has always been one of the primary factors that I considered while I searched for internships. Thus, when I learned about the amazing culture that has been cultivated by a dedicated and passionate community at Southwest Airlines, I became eager to apply.

I have always had a passion for technology. Upon my transfer to Texas A&M University, I quickly gravitated towards the major, Technology Management. However, I quickly realized that real-world experience was absolutely essential for any who was serious about pursuing a career in technology. Consequently, I began working part-time on campus as a Student Technician while formulating an internship plan for my senior year. My goal was to schedule two internships back-to-back, for summer and fall, in order to gain six months of full-time IT experience prior graduation. After securing an internship for the summer of 2018, I began to search for a fall internship. I spoke with a Southwest Airlines representative at Texas A&M University’s Cybersecurity Career Fair. She spoke extensively about how she felt highly satisfied with the work that she was doing, and regarding how Southwest had changed her life. Over the course of four semesters, I have attended nine career fairs at my university. I had never met a company representative who was this passionate about their organization. Thus, I began to research Southwest’s culture. Furthermore, I spoke with classmates, colleagues, and my professor regarding Southwest Airlines. I learned from multiple sources that the organization was highly dedicated to creating a purposeful and compassionate community.

Consequently, I decided to build this website for two reasons. First, I wanted to demonstrate that I enjoy exploring and learning new skills. I acquired the knowledge needed in Django, AWS, and Bootstrap (I edited the JavaScript, CSS, and HTML in the Bootstrap file) in order to build this website. Secondly, I wanted to provide a “Why Southwest?” essay, even if was not requested, because I am extremely appreciative for this opportunity and I firmly believe that actions speak louder than words. Thank you for your time.
